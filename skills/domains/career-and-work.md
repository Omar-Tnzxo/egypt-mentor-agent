# Skill: Career and Work Domain

Use this skill when the user's case touches:

- jobs
- employability
- job search
- career change
- promotion
- workplace performance
- salary growth
- freelancing as work
- role transitions
- managers and team leads
- professional direction in Egypt

## Goal

Filter the recommendation through Egyptian work and career reality instead of generic self-improvement advice.

## Core rule

In career and work decisions, effort alone is not enough.
The agent must distinguish between:

- personal ambition
- market demand
- employer-side value
- visible proof of capability
- execution consistency
- role fit
- compensation reality
- advancement path

Do not assume one default actor inside career and work.
Always identify who the current speaker is, what role they currently occupy, who evaluates them, and what the market is actually rewarding.

## Actor examples in this domain

The speaker may be:

- a student
- a fresh graduate
- a job seeker
- an employee
- a freelancer
- a manager
- a team lead
- a founder who is also evaluating a career path
- a parent helping someone decide
- an HR or talent stakeholder

## Egypt-specific domain realities

- competition for good opportunities can be high, especially for entry-level roles
- market demand matters more than aspiration alone
- private-sector growth is a major source of job creation
- employability often depends on proof of value, not only credentials
- practical skills, consistency, and communication can matter as much as formal qualifications
- many people stay stuck because they optimize for prestige signals instead of actual market fit
- in some cases, the best next move is a narrower, faster path to paid value rather than a grand long-term identity plan

## Reality checks

Always inspect:

- what the user wants
- what the market currently rewards
- what proof of capability already exists
- what proof is missing
- whether the current bottleneck is skills, positioning, execution, confidence, environment, or weak opportunity selection
- whether the target path is realistic given the user's current time, finances, obligations, and runway
- whether the case is about getting in, getting paid more, performing better, switching tracks, or becoming more resilient at work

## Important distinctions

Separate clearly between:

- wanting a path
- qualifying for a path
- signaling fit for a path
- getting interviews or opportunities
- performing well after entry
- long-term career growth

Do not confuse motivation, online learning, or job applications with real employability progress.

## Common useful questions

The agent should often determine:

- who the speaker is in the work system
- what exact outcome matters now: first job, better job, higher income, promotion, career switch, role stability, or freelance income
- what the current marketable asset is
- what the strongest evidence of ability is
- what gap most blocks progress: skill, proof, positioning, network, communication, discipline, or wrong target
- whether the path is employer-led, client-led, credential-led, portfolio-led, or relationship-led
- whether the user is pursuing a realistic entry wedge or chasing a vague identity

## Good near-term paths in this domain

Depending on the case, the agent may lean toward:

- clarifying one target role instead of a broad identity
- building proof of ability before applying more widely
- pursuing a narrower entry path with clearer market demand
- prioritizing income stability before prestige
- improving visible work quality, communication, and consistency before chasing a dramatic switch
- choosing the smallest move that creates real leverage in the Egyptian labor market

## Output requirements

When this skill is active, include:

- primary actor in the current case
- relevant secondary actors
- what outcome the user currently wants
- what the market is likely rewarding in this path
- what proof already exists versus what is still missing
- where the main employability or work-performance leak exists
- what should be tested next in the Egyptian career and work context
