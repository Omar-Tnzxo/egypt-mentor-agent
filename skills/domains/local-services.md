# Skill: Local Services Domain

Use this skill when the user's case touches:

- local service businesses
- home services
- maintenance
- cleaning
- repairs
- car services
- beauty and personal care services
- clinics and appointment-driven local operations
- small local agencies
- on-ground B2B services
- recurring service operations in Egypt

## Goal

Filter the recommendation through Egyptian local-service reality instead of generic small-business advice.

## Core rule

In local services, interest alone is not enough.
The agent must distinguish between:

- inquiry volume
- booking intent
- show-up reliability
- service quality
- repeat business
- referral strength
- capacity utilization
- unit economics

Do not assume one default actor inside local services.
Always identify who the current speaker is, who receives the service, who pays, who schedules, and where the real operational bottleneck sits.

## Actor examples in this domain

The speaker may be:

- a solo operator
- a clinic owner
- a service manager
- a technician
- a field worker
- an operations lead
- a local business owner
- a dispatcher or scheduler
- a marketer for a local service business
- a customer

## Egypt-specific domain realities

- trust, response speed, and reliability often matter more than polished branding
- many local-service businesses grow through referrals, WhatsApp, phone calls, and neighborhood reputation
- demand may exist, but operational inconsistency can kill retention and word of mouth
- price sensitivity can be high, but unreliability can cost more than price in many categories
- capacity limits, staff quality, and scheduling discipline often matter as much as lead generation
- many service businesses mistake more inquiries for growth when the real leak is no-show rates, weak follow-up, or inconsistent delivery

## Reality checks

Always inspect:

- who the buyer is
- who the end user is
- what makes the service trusted
- how bookings currently happen
- what causes missed appointments, slow response, or customer drop-off
- whether demand is one-off, recurring, emergency-driven, convenience-driven, or relationship-driven
- whether the current team and operations can absorb more volume without breaking quality
- whether growth is blocked by acquisition, response time, scheduling, delivery quality, or repeat rate

## Important distinctions

Separate clearly between:

- attention
- inquiries
- booked work
- completed work
- repeat work
- referral-driven growth
- healthy service margin

Do not confuse message volume, calls, or page activity with strong local-service performance.

## Common useful questions

The agent should often determine:

- who the main actor is in the service system
- what exact outcome matters now: more bookings, better retention, higher utilization, better margins, stronger referrals, or more stable operations
- where the main leak is: lead quality, response time, scheduling, no-shows, delivery quality, follow-up, or staff reliability
- whether the model depends more on trust, convenience, urgency, location, or recurring need
- whether the business is operator-led, team-led, brand-led, platform-led, or referral-led
- whether the business should grow volume now or first improve execution consistency

## Good near-term paths in this domain

Depending on the case, the agent may lean toward:

- tightening response and booking flow before spending more on acquisition
- improving service consistency before expanding the team
- focusing on one high-trust service line before broadening the offer
- improving repeat and referral systems before chasing cold demand
- clarifying who schedules, who delivers, and who owns follow-up
- simplifying operations so growth does not destroy service quality

## Output requirements

When this skill is active, include:

- primary actor in the current case
- relevant secondary actors
- where the strongest current value exists
- where the main local-service leak exists
- what part of the operation most threatens trust or margin
- what monetization or growth path is most plausible right now
- what should be tested next in the Egyptian local-services context
