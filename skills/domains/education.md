# Skill: Education Domain

Use this skill when the user's case touches:

- schools
- tutoring
- training centers
- online courses
- cohort-based education
- test preparation
- university-related services
- upskilling
- education operations in Egypt
- edtech products

## Goal

Filter the recommendation through Egyptian education reality instead of generic content-business logic.

## Core rule

In education, interest alone is not enough.
The agent must distinguish between:

- learner motivation
- buyer motivation
- enrollment
- attendance
- completion
- learning outcomes
- career or practical outcomes
- institutional value

Do not assume one default actor inside education.
Always identify who the current speaker is, who learns, who pays, who decides, and who measures success.

## Actor examples in this domain

The speaker may be:

- a founder
- a school owner
- a training center operator
- a teacher
- a tutor
- a parent
- a student
- a university staff member
- an admissions or operations lead
- an HR or L&D stakeholder
- a creator building an education product

## Egypt-specific domain realities

- trust, reputation, and proof of outcomes matter heavily
- the learner is not always the buyer
- parents, employers, institutions, or sponsors may shape the decision
- many education products win through clarity, discipline, and results, not just content volume
- attendance and completion can collapse if structure is weak
- exam pressure, credentials, and employability can outweigh abstract learning value
- in many cases, operational consistency matters as much as curriculum quality

## Reality checks

Always inspect:

- who the real learner is
- who pays
- who decides
- what outcome the buyer actually wants
- whether the product is outcome-driven, credential-driven, convenience-driven, or support-driven
- whether the learner returns consistently or drops off
- whether the experience depends on live support, accountability, content quality, operations, or community
- whether the model works for schools, direct learners, parents, institutions, or employers

## Important distinctions

Separate clearly between:

- interest
- enrollment
- attendance
- completion
- real learning
- measurable outcomes
- monetization readiness

Do not confuse registrations, views, or student counts with strong education value.

## Common useful questions

The agent should often determine:

- who the main actor is in the education system
- whether the learner and buyer are the same person
- what exact outcome matters most: grades, exam success, skills, certification, placement, retention, or operational efficiency
- where the main leak is: acquisition, trust, enrollment, attendance, completion, outcomes, or renewal
- whether the offer depends on content, instructor quality, accountability, community, or institutional integration
- whether the business is B2C, parent-led, school-led, employer-led, or hybrid
- whether success depends more on pedagogy, operations, distribution, or credibility

## Good near-term paths in this domain

Depending on the case, the agent may lean toward:

- proving one strong learner outcome before expanding catalog breadth
- improving attendance and completion before chasing more enrollments
- targeting the true buyer more directly instead of only the learner
- reducing the offer to one high-value education outcome
- strengthening accountability and structure before scaling content volume
- clarifying whether the model is consumer education, institutional education, or career-oriented training

## Output requirements

When this skill is active, include:

- primary actor in the current case
- relevant secondary actors
- where the strongest current value exists
- where the main education leak exists
- what kind of outcome is already proven versus still assumed
- where monetization is most likely to come from
- what should be tested next in the Egyptian education context
