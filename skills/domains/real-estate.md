# Skill: Real Estate Domain

Use this skill when the user's case touches:

- property sales
- brokerage
- developers
- compounds
- resale
- real-estate lead generation
- proptech
- real-estate operations in Egypt

## Goal

Filter the recommendation through Egyptian real-estate market reality instead of generic startup logic.

## Core rule

In Egyptian real estate, attention is not the same as trust, and usage is not the same as monetizable value.
The agent must distinguish between:

- broker-side value
- company-side value
- developer-side value
- buyer-side value
- manager-side value
- operations-side value

Do not assume one default actor inside the real-estate sector.
Always identify who the current speaker is and which actor matters most in the current case.

## Actor examples in this domain

The speaker may be:

- an individual sales agent
- a team leader
- a sales manager
- a brokerage owner
- a developer employee
- a marketer
- an operations person
- a buyer
- a renter
- a technical founder building a proptech product

## Reality checks

Always inspect:

- who exactly gets value
- who can actually pay
- whether the workflow is daily, weekly, or occasional
- whether the product improves sales execution, lead handling, team visibility, reporting, coordination, or conversion
- whether the market segment is primary sales, resale, rentals, or internal company operations
- whether the product depends on behavior change from brokers, managers, companies, or end customers

## Egypt-specific domain realities

- many users may sign up out of curiosity, not commitment
- sales teams often operate through informal habits, WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and personal follow-up
- companies may pay more easily for workflow control, reporting, and lead visibility than for a generic app
- broker usage alone may not produce revenue if company-side value is unclear
- trust, relationships, and offline follow-up still matter heavily
- enterprise-style selling may be slower, but often more monetizable than pure consumer traction

## Important distinctions

Separate clearly between:

- user growth
- active operational usage
- business value delivered
- monetization readiness

Do not confuse reported user counts with product-market fit.

## Common useful questions

The agent should often determine:

- who the speaker is in the real-estate system
- which actor is the main target actor
- whether the active users are agents, managers, buyers, companies, or mixed
- what exact workflow they use repeatedly
- what painful step becomes easier because of the product
- whether the company or decision-maker sees measurable ROI
- whether there is a path to B2B monetization, actor-specific monetization, or workflow-based monetization

## Good near-term paths in this domain

Depending on the case, the agent may lean toward:

- workflow validation before growth
- B2B monetization before mass user expansion
- targeting sales managers instead of individual agents
- targeting one actor type instead of a mixed audience
- proving one ROI-heavy use case first
- reducing the product to one critical operational pain point

## Output requirements

When this skill is active, include:

- primary actor in the current case
- relevant secondary actors
- what kind of value is currently proven
- what kind of value is still assumed
- where monetization is most likely to come from
- what should be tested next in the Egyptian market context
