# Skill: Ecommerce Domain

Use this skill when the user's case touches:

- online retail
- D2C brands
- marketplaces
- social commerce
- online catalog sales
- fulfillment
- conversion optimization
- ecommerce operations in Egypt

## Goal

Filter the recommendation through Egyptian ecommerce reality instead of generic internet-business logic.

## Core rule

In ecommerce, traffic alone is not enough.
The agent must distinguish between:

- attention
- intent
- conversion
- fulfillment quality
- repeat purchase behavior
- margin health
- payment reliability

Do not assume one default actor inside ecommerce.
Always identify who the current speaker is, who buys, who operates, who owns the margin, and where the real bottleneck sits.

## Actor examples in this domain

The speaker may be:

- a founder
- a brand owner
- an ecommerce manager
- a performance marketer
- an operations lead
- a merchant
- a marketplace seller
- a customer support lead
- a buyer
- a logistics or fulfillment operator

## Egypt-specific domain realities

- price sensitivity is often high
- trust and reliability can matter as much as the product itself
- payment behavior may still include COD, wallets, cards, or installment options depending on the category
- logistics quality and delivery expectations differ strongly by city and category
- social discovery and informal selling channels can matter a lot
- repeat purchase economics may be very different from first-order growth
- many ecommerce problems are operational, not only marketing problems

## Reality checks

Always inspect:

- who the buyer is
- what makes them trust the purchase
- what causes drop-off before payment
- what happens after the order is placed
- whether the product category supports repeat buying
- whether margin survives ads, delivery, returns, and discounts
- whether the current channel matches the buyer journey

## Important distinctions

Separate clearly between:

- traffic growth
- conversion growth
- fulfillment quality
- repeat purchase strength
- contribution margin
- sustainable revenue

Do not confuse order volume, page visits, or follower growth with healthy ecommerce performance.

## Common useful questions

The agent should often determine:

- who the main actor is in the ecommerce system
- where the main leak is: traffic, conversion, checkout, fulfillment, returns, or repeat purchase
- whether the business is marketplace-led, direct-store-led, social-commerce-led, or hybrid
- whether the category is trust-heavy, convenience-heavy, or price-heavy
- whether the current economics can survive scaling
- whether growth is blocked by payments, logistics, margin, trust, or weak offer clarity

## Good near-term paths in this domain

Depending on the case, the agent may lean toward:

- fixing conversion before buying more traffic
- improving checkout trust before scaling ads
- improving fulfillment and post-purchase experience before chasing volume
- reducing category sprawl and focusing on winners
- improving repeat purchase systems before broad expansion
- clarifying contribution margin before treating volume as success

## Output requirements

When this skill is active, include:

- primary actor in the current case
- relevant secondary actors
- where the strongest current value exists
- where the main ecommerce leak exists
- what part of the model threatens margin or trust most
- what should be tested next in the Egyptian ecommerce context
