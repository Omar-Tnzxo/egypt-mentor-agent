# Skill: Fintech Domain

Use this skill when the user's case touches:

- digital payments
- collections
- bill payments
- lending workflows
- BNPL
- expense management
- payroll-linked finance
- merchant finance
- digital banking
- financial operations in Egypt

## Goal

Filter the recommendation through Egyptian fintech reality instead of generic software logic.

## Core rule

In fintech, user convenience alone is not enough.
The agent must distinguish between:

- user-side value
- merchant-side value
- company-side value
- finance-team value
- partner-bank or regulated-institution value
- compliance and trust requirements

Do not assume one default actor inside fintech.
Always identify who the current speaker is, who receives the value, who pays, and who controls risk.

## Actor examples in this domain

The speaker may be:

- a founder
- a product builder
- an operations lead
- a finance team member
- an MSME owner
- a merchant
- an end user
- a lender-side operator
- a payments or collections manager
- a partner inside a regulated institution

## Egypt-specific domain realities

- trust and perceived safety matter heavily
- regulation, licensing, and partnerships can shape what is possible
- many useful fintech products win by reducing friction in existing financial behavior, not by trying to replace the whole system at once
- MSMEs and operational teams may value better collections, visibility, reconciliation, and control more than abstract innovation
- distribution, onboarding friction, and compliance burden can become growth bottlenecks
- in some cases, institutional adoption may matter more than pure consumer signups

## Reality checks

Always inspect:

- who gets immediate value
- who bears risk
- who can approve payment or adoption
- whether the product improves a real financial workflow
- whether the workflow is frequent enough to matter
- whether trust and compliance friction are manageable
- whether the use case is B2C, B2B, B2B2C, or infrastructure-like

## Important distinctions

Separate clearly between:

- user activity
- transaction activity
- monetizable activity
- regulated capability
- operational viability

Do not confuse interest, downloads, or app installs with sustainable fintech value.

## Common useful questions

The agent should often determine:

- who the main actor is in the financial workflow
- who the economic buyer is
- what exact financial pain is being reduced
- whether the product saves time, reduces risk, improves control, or increases conversion
- whether the current value is strong enough for adoption and payment
- whether growth is blocked by trust, regulation, channel, onboarding, or weak value clarity

## Good near-term paths in this domain

Depending on the case, the agent may lean toward:

- solving one painful workflow first
- proving operational value before broad expansion
- targeting MSME or enterprise pain before mass consumer growth
- clarifying the regulated or partner-dependent parts of the model early
- selling control, reconciliation, or efficiency before selling a broad financial platform vision

## Output requirements

When this skill is active, include:

- primary actor in the current case
- relevant secondary actors
- where the strongest value currently exists
- where trust or compliance friction is highest
- where monetization is most likely to come from
- what should be validated next in the Egyptian fintech context
