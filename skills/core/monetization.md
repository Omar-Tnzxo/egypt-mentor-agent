# Skill: Monetization

Use this skill when the user has:

- a product with users but no revenue
- a service with unclear pricing
- demand without a capture mechanism
- traction without a buyer strategy
- confusion about pricing, packaging, or business model

## Goal

Help the agent identify how value can be captured financially without forcing monetization before the case is ready.

## Core rule

Do not ask how to make money from something before you know:

- who gets real value
- who can pay
- what exactly they would be paying for
- why paying is rational now

Monetization is not a button.
It is a value-capture decision built on validated pain, a clear buyer, and a believable payment reason.

## What to inspect

- who receives the value
- who feels the pain strongly enough to pay
- who controls budget or payment approval
- whether the buyer is the same as the user
- whether the current product creates a measurable outcome
- whether the outcome is strong enough to package and price
- whether monetization should be direct, indirect, B2B, B2C, or hybrid

## Readiness check

Before recommending a monetization move, inspect:

### 1. Value clarity

Can the product clearly say what it improves?
Examples:

- saves time
- improves conversion
- increases visibility
- reduces manual work
- improves control or reporting
- shortens response time

### 2. Buyer clarity

Identify:

- the real buyer
- the budget owner
- the approval path
- the payment motive

### 3. Payment logic

Why would the buyer pay now instead of later or never?
Possible reasons:

- immediate ROI
- pain reduction
- team control
- competitive pressure
- revenue upside
- workflow dependency

### 4. Packaging logic

What is the most natural thing to charge for?
Examples:

- per seat
- per team
- per company
- per workflow
- per result
- setup plus subscription
- service plus software

### 5. Friction check

What could block payment?
Examples:

- weak trust
- unclear ROI
- wrong buyer
- budget friction
- low dependency
- easy workaround
- product not tied to a painful enough process

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- pricing before validation
- charging random users just because there is activity
- confusing signups with willingness to pay
- adding many pricing tiers before finding the right buyer
- chasing monetization from the wrong actor in the system

## If monetization is weak

The agent should consider:

- changing the buyer target
- narrowing the use case
- selling one ROI-heavy workflow first
- moving toward B2B if company value is stronger than end-user value
- using paid pilots before full pricing complexity
- pairing service with product if pure software value is still weak

## Output requirements

When this skill is active, include:

- current monetization readiness
- likely buyer
- why that buyer may pay
- strongest value-to-payment link
- biggest monetization blocker
- best next monetization test
