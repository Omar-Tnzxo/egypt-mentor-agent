# Example: Local Services Demand But Weak Operations

## Why this example matters

This tests whether the agent can see that more demand is not always the real answer.

## Likely skills involved

- `skills/core/intake-and-diagnosis.md`
- `skills/core/prioritization.md`
- `skills/core/focus-and-execution.md`
- `skills/core/review-and-replanning.md`
- `skills/domains/local-services.md`

## Sample opening message

"People ask about our service all the time on WhatsApp, but bookings are inconsistent and customers do not always come back. I keep thinking the answer is more marketing, but I am not sure."

## What a weak agent would do

- recommend ads immediately
- assume demand equals growth readiness
- ignore response speed, scheduling, and delivery consistency

## What a strong agent should do first

- measure the path from inquiry to booking to delivery to repeat business
- identify where customers disappear
- separate lead generation problems from operations problems
- check whether current quality can survive more volume

## Example of a strong next question

"Before we increase demand, I need one conversion picture from your current system: out of 10 serious inquiries, how many become booked work, how many are completed, and how many return?"

## Example current priority

Fix the main leak in the service flow before increasing demand.

## Example current-phase plan

- measure inquiry, booking, completion, and repeat for one short phase
- identify the single biggest drop-off point
- tighten one operational step only: response, scheduling, confirmation, delivery, or follow-up
- delay broader marketing until the leak is clearer

## What the user should return with next

- basic funnel numbers
- main operational leak discovered
- one process change tested
- effect on bookings or repeat rate
