# Example: Founder With Users But No Revenue

## Why this example matters

This tests whether the agent can avoid shallow startup advice when a founder has traction signals but no business progress.

## Sample opening message

"I have an Egyptian real-estate app. It has been live for a year and has more than 2,000 users, but there is no revenue and no real progress. I have another full-time job and I do not know what to focus on next."

## What a weak agent would do

- tell the user to market harder
- suggest ads immediately
- suggest fundraising
- treat user count as proof of product-market fit
- dump a giant startup roadmap

## What a strong agent should do first

- identify who the active users actually are
- identify who gets value and who can pay
- determine whether the speaker is solving a user problem, a manager problem, or a company problem
- ask for evidence about active use, repeated workflows, and current bottlenecks
- avoid giving a monetization plan before clarifying whether value is real and to whom

## Example of a strong next question

"My current understanding is that you have usage signals but not yet proven business value. Before I suggest anything, I need to know who is getting repeat value today: individual agents, sales managers, brokerages, or buyers?"

## Example validated case summary

- Goal: turn the app into a viable business
- Current reality: user count exists, revenue does not
- Constraints: limited time, limited money, founder also has a full-time job
- What has been tried: product has been built and used for a year
- Current results: more than 2,000 users, but monetization is still unclear
- Bottleneck: unclear paying actor and unclear proven ROI
- Stage goal: identify one monetizable actor and one repeat pain point

## Example current priority

Prove which actor receives enough operational value to justify payment.

## Why this is the priority

Without a clear paying actor and ROI-heavy pain point, more growth only increases noise.
The immediate question is not how to scale the app.
The immediate question is whether there is one narrow use case worth monetizing.

## What is not the priority now

- broad user growth
- investor storytelling
- feature expansion for mixed audiences
- brand awareness campaigns

## Example current-phase plan

- interview 8 to 10 current active users from one actor type only
- classify each interview by actor, workflow, repeated pain, and willingness to pay
- identify one workflow that happens frequently enough to matter
- write one monetization hypothesis tied to that workflow
- ignore all other expansion ideas until this is clear

## Example execution shape

- one interview block during two evenings this week
- one synthesis session on the weekend
- one written hypothesis by the end of the phase

## Example review trigger

Review once the interviews are complete or once it becomes obvious that no actor has strong enough value.

## What the user should return with next

- actor types interviewed
- repeated workflow observed
- top pain points mentioned
- signs of willingness to pay
- contradictions between user types
